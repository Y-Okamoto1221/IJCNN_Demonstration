Onoma-to-wave: Environmental sound sysnthesis from onomatopoeic words 　　


Author: Yuki Okamoto, Keisuke Imoto, Shinnosuke Takamichi, Ryosuke Yamanishi, Takahiro Fukumori, Yoichi Yamashita

Paper: https://arxiv.org/abs/2102.05872

This is a demonstration of environmental sound synthesis from onomatopoeic words [1]. As sounds of the dataset, we used 10 different sound events (manual coffee grinder, cup clinking, alarm clock ringing, whistle, maracas, drum, electric shave, trash box banging, tearing paper, bell ringing) contained in the RWCP-SSD (Real World Computing Partnership-sound Scene Database) [2]. For the onomatopoeic words corresponding to each sound sample, we used the dataset in RWCP-SSD-Onomatopoeia [3].
